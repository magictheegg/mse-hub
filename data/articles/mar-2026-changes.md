### Ardleby Gatekeeper
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/007ff1efc5f9ee23b78d312f851d137aa04c2eb7/sets/HOD-files/img/128_Ardleby%20Gatekeeper.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/ad57f99e8004beacc283154725f43a316024b9ab/sets/HOD-files/img/128_Ardleby%20Gatekeeper.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Gatekeeper presents a must-answer threat on turn 3 due to its ability to keep cloning itself to make an infinite stream of bodies. If it isn't removed at instant speed, it also generates immedate value, but because of how devastating it is to let ardleby live even a turn it forces the opponent to 2 for 1 themselves. If the opponent isn't able to immediately answer the gatekeeper, it creates a copy of itself and is further insulated from removal, requiring the opponent to spend multiple removal spells to stop the endless stream of bodies.

### Prophellos, Hungering Manipulator
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/007ff1efc5f9ee23b78d312f851d137aa04c2eb7/sets/FOE-files/img/15_Prophellos,%20Hungering%20Manipulator.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/ad57f99e8004beacc283154725f43a316024b9ab/sets/FOE-files/img/15_Prophellos,%20Hungering%20Manipulator.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Prophellos was simply one of the cheapest costed and easiest to break delve cards in both canon and custom. It could come down on 0 mana after an [[Algaja, Lord of Disregard]] and double your board presence, while also being able to sometimes come out of the graveyard. This change has been made to bring it more in line with the rest of the format (and canon) and prevent it from being as much of a timebomb.

### Opportunistic Scavenger
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/007ff1efc5f9ee23b78d312f851d137aa04c2eb7/sets/VNM-files/img/242_Opportunistic%20Scavenger.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/ad57f99e8004beacc283154725f43a316024b9ab/sets/VNM-files/img/242_Opportunistic%20Scavenger.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Being able to loop Scavenger with [[Lifelighter]] and similar self-bounce effects was both likely too powerful (with how good recurring 2 things is) and led to miserable gamestates. Recurring removal has been a consistent issue in voyager up until very recently, and we're willing to be quite strict on it as it's a play pattern that isn't enjoyable and one we don't want to see again.

</right-card>

### Drakesteel Effigy
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/007ff1efc5f9ee23b78d312f851d137aa04c2eb7/sets/FOE-files/img/332_Drakesteel%20Effigy.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/ad57f99e8004beacc283154725f43a316024b9ab/sets/FOE-files/img/332_Drakesteel%20Effigy.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Drakesteel Effigy was previously far more powerful than any comparable effect in canon magic, becoming a stabilizing card advantage engine with a trivial condition. Getting to 6 differently named lands, although not something you'd do by accident, is not difficult enough for how powerful Drakesteel is, and the failcase of having to pay a small bit of mana was still extremely powerful. These two factors combined, as well as the extreme popularity of the card, led to use making this change.

### Eteres, Planar Analyst
<change-preview>
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/007ff1efc5f9ee23b78d312f851d137aa04c2eb7/sets/WAW-files/img/191_Eteres,%20Planar%20Analyst.jpg?raw=true
https://github.com/Voyager-MTG/Voyager-MTG.github.io/blob/ad57f99e8004beacc283154725f43a316024b9ab/sets/WAW-files/img/191_Eteres,%20Planar%20Analyst.jpg?raw=true
</change-preview>

Eteres in combination with [[Motel Manager]] creates a fast 2 card combo win we don't want to exist in the format. This is not only our earliest 2 card combo (it can happen as early as turn 3 with the help of 1drop acceleration), eteres can also functionally act as a 1 card combo. Eteres can exile motel manager and the required number of lands, than immediately -2 to get the manager. If one of the exiled lands was a [[Windy Weft]], you win the next turn (on turn 5 assuming no acceleration to get eteres out). The consistency and speed eteres offers in an insta-kill combo is not something we believe should be in the format. The main issue here is the fact that eteres does an effect (mana severance) that is paid off by other combo pieces, but then also tutors those combo pieces.

</right-card>
